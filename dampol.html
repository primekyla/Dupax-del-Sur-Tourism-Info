Dampol Bridge
A Closer Look

The Dampol Bridge is one of Dupax del Sur’s treasured historical landmarks. Built during the Spanish colonial period using adobe stones and lime, this centuries-old bridge has withstood the test of time and remains a symbol of the town’s enduring heritage. Stretching gracefully over the Abanatan River, it once served as a vital passageway for travelers and traders, connecting communities and fostering growth in the area. Today, the Dampol Bridge stands not only as a functional structure but also as a living reminder of history, inviting visitors to walk across its stones and experience a direct link to the past.
